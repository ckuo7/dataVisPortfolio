Because of their accessibility and affordability, more than 500 bike sharing programs are running around the world to provide a low-cost alternative for short-term commutes and to help alleviate traffic. As bike sharing demands increase, unavailability and uneven distribution of bikes become a critical issue for the programs, which could undermine usersâ€™ experience and waste public resources.
    This project applied the most well known data visualization tool - D3 on the Bikeshare demand data. One of the most important goal of this project is to visualize bike rental demand overtime and how it correlates with extraneous factors such as temperature and weather. (if time is permitted, I will visualize data in terms of network or geolocation with second data resource) The result of this project can serve as part of city mobility research and provides insights of how bike rental demands vary across different time and space.
