  • The dataset contain will contain numerical, categorical, time series, and geolocation data
  • Bike sharing systems have gained popularity over the past decade. Because of their accessibility and affordability, more than 500 bike sharing programs are running around the world to provide a low-cost alternative for short-term commutes and to help alleviate traffic. As bike sharing demands increase, unavailability and uneven distribution of bikes become a critical issue for the programs, which could undermine usersâ€™ experience and waste public resources.
    This project applied the most well known data visualization tool - D3 on the trip history data of the Capital Bikeshare. One of the most important goal of this project is to visualize the uneven distribution of the bike rental demand and supply in different part of the city under various extraneous conditions such as temperature, weather and daypart. The result of this project can serve as part of city mobility research and provides insights of how bike rental demands vary across different time and space.
