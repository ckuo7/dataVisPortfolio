Design Guide

My design principle for this series of visualizations is to make them as simple as possible. Following this rule, I decided not to encode the forth variable with color attribute and make the area less distinguishable in the bubble chart so that we can pay more attention on the correlation between "hsGrad" and "Life Expectancy". To preserve detail information, I created tool tips to provide more context about each data point. The same philosophy is implemented in the heat map and small multiple. To easily decipher the color scale of each level in heat map, I made the stroke of rects and and their corresponding legends thicker so we can associate each color to a real number in the legend. This eases the pain when we try to make comparisons between similar color. As for small multiple, I recalcualte the domain for each subplot to avoid the predominance of some big numbers and, hence, increase the data ink ratio. In addition, I made the y-axis of small multiple visible only when we hovered the subplot. This just make the small multiple cleaner, more concise, and perhaps more easily to focus on the color attribute, which helps to make comparisons between different groups. For example, one can easily tell that the murder rate in east south central is generally higher than New England.The parallel coordinates are relatively complicate. However, with brushing, we can just easily focus on the data we are interested by filtering.
